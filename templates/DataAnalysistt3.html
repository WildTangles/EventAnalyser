tt¯ decay - jets 

Among the decay products in the tt¯ decay channel are also between 2-6 quarks with 2-4 bottom quarks. We can further isolate the tt¯ decay signal from the background processes by applying this additional cut!

At ATLAS, quarks are detected by proxy. A jet (narrow cone of hadrons and other particles) results from the hadronization of these quarks and jet count is therefore an indicator of original quark count. Notice that so far, the dataset has been dominated by W and Z events as these are the most likely to occur. Fortunately, these W and Z events only occur with 0 or 4 jets. In addition, to make a cut specifically for bottom quarks, we'll rely on jet flavor tagging. b-tagged jets are jets that were identified to originate from bottom quarks. Finally, it's generally preferred not to make cuts that are unnecessarily strict as this can shrink the dataset too much! Let's further isolate the tt¯ decay events from the background signal by adding an additional cut for 2-9 jets and 2-4 b-tagged jets.
